# Content Strategy Reference

Actionable frameworks for planning, prioritizing, and structuring SEO content.

---

## 1. Three-Bucket SEO Model

Modern visibility requires presence across three channels simultaneously:

**Bucket 1 -- Traditional SEO (Google organic)**
- Still the dominant traffic source; foundation for everything else
- 70% of AI overview sources come from Google's top 10 organic results
- If you rank well in traditional search, you are far more likely to appear in AI answers

**Bucket 2 -- AI Visibility (LLM citations + AI overviews)**
- AI tools do web searches to answer questions; top Google results get cited most
- Being *mentioned* in an AI answer is often more valuable than being cited as a source
- Exhaustive topical coverage increases your odds of becoming a "core source" (consistently cited across related queries)
- Money pages must include granular product/service details -- AI pulls specific facts, not just benefit statements

**Bucket 3 -- Owned Audience (email, community, media)**
- Email list is the only traffic source immune to algorithm changes
- Every platform you rely on runs on algorithms that can change overnight
- Use lead magnets tied to your content topics to convert visitors into subscribers
- Communities (forums, social) build trust that compounds over time
- Consider building a media layer (podcast, newsletter, video) on top of your content engine

**Key principle:** Never put all traffic in one basket. Build the SEO engine first, then diversify.

## 2. Six-Month Content Plan Framework

Layer content production across four types, roughly in this order of priority:

### Layer 1 -- Money Pages (Month 1-2)
- Service pages, product pages, pricing pages, location pages
- These are bottom-of-funnel; convert visitors directly
- Make them longer and more detailed than you think necessary -- AI systems pull specific feature/benefit details
- Include explicit answers to late-stage buyer questions (integrations, requirements, pricing tiers)
- Average high-converting bottom-of-funnel page can be as short as 400-500 words if highly specific

### Layer 2 -- Topic Clusters (Month 2-4)
- Build clusters around your money pages
- One pillar asset per cluster, then supporting pages targeting long-tail variations
- Target keywords where sites of similar authority already rank (compete in your weight class)
- Cover competitor-specific keywords: "[competitor] alternatives", "[competitor] vs [you]", brand-specific sub-clusters
- Use content gap analysis: find keywords competitors rank for where you do not

### Layer 3 -- Link Bait + Free Tools (Month 3-5)
- Free tools attract backlinks naturally and are immune to AI overview click theft (no AI overview appears for tool queries)
- Build micro-tools using AI coding -- one specific problem, solved well
- Tools that rank can earn thousands of backlinks and hundreds of thousands of monthly visits
- Data-driven studies, original research, and experience-driven stories earn press links organically

### Layer 4 -- Top-of-Funnel + Authority Content (Month 4-6)
- Informational content that attracts your target audience earlier in their journey
- Product-led content marketing: embed product recommendations naturally within educational content
- Repurpose existing blog content into other formats (video, social, podcast)

## 3. Topic Opportunity Scoring

Evaluate every keyword/topic against four criteria before committing:

1. **Search demand** -- Consistent monthly searches; check for seasonal patterns in sparkline charts
2. **Rankability** -- Sites of similar domain authority already appear in the top 5; set an upper DR threshold (your DR + 10)
3. **Business value** -- Would someone searching this be interested in your product/service? Score 0-3
4. **Content gap** -- Are competitors covering this poorly or not at all? Weak competition = fast wins

**Prioritize:** Keywords that score well on all four. Start with low-competition, high-business-value terms. Work up to heavy hitters as your authority grows.

## 4. Content Funnel Structure

### Top of Funnel (ToFu) -- Awareness
- "What is", "how to", broad educational queries
- Goal: attract target audience, build email list, earn brand awareness
- Monetize with display ads (paid per pageview) + email capture
- Accept that AI overviews may steal some clicks here -- these were often low-conversion clicks anyway

### Middle of Funnel (MoFu) -- Consideration
- "Best [category]", "[X] vs [Y]", comparison guides, reviews
- Goal: position your product/service among alternatives
- Product-led content: recommend your product naturally alongside genuine advice
- Get featured in third-party roundup/listicle posts for AI visibility

### Bottom of Funnel (BoFu) -- Decision
- "[Product] pricing", "[service] near me", "buy [product]"
- Goal: convert directly
- Landing pages, not blog posts
- Include detailed specs, integrations, pricing -- the facts AI systems look for
- Own your brand SERP: rank #1 for "[your brand] alternatives" to control the narrative

## 5. Content Refresh Cycle

### When to Refresh
- Page ranking positions 4-20 (close to page 1 but not there yet)
- Content score below 70 when compared against current top-ranking pages
- Traffic declining gradually over weeks/months (content decay signal)
- Outdated stats, deprecated methods, stale pricing
- Competitor has published stronger content on same topic

### Refresh Process
1. Pull Google Search Console data for last 28 days
2. Filter keywords in positions 3-20
3. Identify keywords you rank for but do not optimize for on the ranking page
4. Write new sections for those keywords, add to existing sections, move key terms higher on the page
5. Update outdated information, add new examples/data
6. Re-optimize heading hierarchy and internal links
7. Monitor ranking changes over next 60 days

### Refresh vs. New Content
- Refreshing existing content that already ranks (positions 3-20) yields faster results than publishing new content
- Stop publishing new posts if you have hundreds of existing pages getting zero traffic -- fix those first
- Audit regularly: flag pages with declining impressions before traffic loss becomes severe

## 6. Programmatic SEO Guidance

### When It Works
- Targeting hundreds/thousands of similar long-tail keywords with a repeatable template
- Location pages, comparison pages, directory listings, tool pages
- Data-driven pages where the structure is consistent but the content varies

### When It Fails
- Pages are thin or undifferentiated (same template with just a city name swapped)
- Mass AI-generated content at scale -- "Mount AI" pattern: traffic spikes then collapses
- Google's scaled content abuse policy specifically targets this
- Even sophisticated AI-scaled content gets detected; sites have gone from millions of clicks to near-zero

### Safe Approach
- Every programmatic page must satisfy unique search intent
- Add genuinely unique data, local details, or editorial content per page
- Keep total page count reasonable; quality over quantity
- Monitor for indexation issues -- pages without traction should be removed or consolidated

## 7. Seasonal and Event Content Strategy

### Seasonal Content
- Prepare content well before the seasonal wave hits (months ahead)
- Use keyword sparkline charts to spot recurring annual patterns
- Pre-optimize and publish strong content before competitors can catch up
- Examples: gardening tips peak in spring; survival/prepping spikes during crises

### Event-Driven Content
- Some keywords spike dramatically during specific world events (natural disasters, elections, crises)
- Having pre-existing, high-quality content on these topics means traffic surges come to you
- Monitor trending topics in your niche and have cornerstone content ready

### How to Find Opportunities
- Enter seed keywords into a keyword research tool
- Scan sparkline charts for recurring spikes or one-off surges
- These keywords are often lower competition because few people optimize for non-evergreen terms

## 8. Content Risk Diversification

### Platform Risks
- Google algorithm updates can wipe out traffic overnight
- AI overviews are reducing clicks for informational queries (up to 34.5% CTR reduction for position 1)
- Social platforms can change algorithms or shadow ban without warning

### Mitigation Strategies
- Build an email list -- the one channel you fully own
- Diversify to 2-3 traffic sources (SEO + YouTube + email is a strong combination)
- Repurpose SEO content across platforms (blog post to video, thread, podcast)
- Choose platforms where your target audience spends time AND where effort-to-reward ratio favors you
- Acquire strategic assets: dormant sites, micro-tools, or media properties with existing authority

### Revenue Diversification
- Do not rely solely on one monetization method (e.g., just affiliate or just ads)
- Layer display ads (paid per pageview) + affiliate (paid per conversion) + lead generation
- Negotiate affiliate rates as traffic grows -- rates are almost always negotiable
- Diversify affiliate partners away from a single merchant
- Traffic is not just money; it is leverage for better deals and partnerships
