# Link Building Reference

## 1. Why Links Still Matter (Data)

- During the DOJ trial, a Google engineer testified under oath that engagement (clicks, bounce-backs) is the #1 ranking signal; links were top-five but not top-three
- A site acquiring a $3,500 domain with strong backlinks saw exponential organic growth immediately after 301 redirects were applied -- proof that link authority still drives rankings
- A test on 10 identical pages showed every page with outbound links outranked those without -- linking out is a net positive, not a loss of authority
- "Link ghosts" are real: in a controlled test, 22 links were added to two sites, then removed months later; every linked page continued to rank considerably higher than its initial position
- Ranking for keywords without pogo-sticking builds authority on its own -- links accelerate this but are not always strictly required for long-tail terms
- One commenter reported outranking strong link profiles on 90% of clients with zero active link building -- by relying on relevance, topical authority, and site architecture instead

## 2. Reverse Engineering Competitor Backlinks

- Use Site Explorer on competitor domains; open the "Best Links" report to see high-quality contextual links
- Look at WHY each link exists: what phrase or context triggered the link (e.g., "flip a coin" in game rules articles)
- Search a database of billions of pages for "footprints" -- phrases competitors' audiences naturally mention
- Identify which link types competitors have (news, blogs, directories, forums) and find the gaps in YOUR profile
- Example: a coin-flip site's best links came from board game rules pages recommending the tool -- replicable by finding every "rules" article mentioning coin flips
- Use the Content Gap report to find keywords competitors rank for that you don't -- then create content targeting those gaps
- Check competitor anchor text distribution to understand what a natural profile looks like in your niche
- Look at Traffic Share by Domains for your target keyword set to see which sites eat the most traffic -- then study their link profiles

## 3. Outdated Statistic Play

- Find heavily-linked statistics in your niche (e.g., "93% of online experiences begin with a search engine")
- Research whether the stat is still accurate -- that particular stat dropped to ~68%
- Email every site linking to the outdated stat, offering the corrected number with a source link to your page
- Place the most linkable stat near the top of your article for maximum visibility
- This tactic took a brand-new page to #1 in under a month
- Use backlink analysis tools to filter by anchor text containing the specific stat -- this gives you a ready-made outreach list
- The pitch practically writes itself: "That stat is 14 years old; the real number today is X -- here's the source"
- Works in any niche where old data is frequently cited (marketing stats, health data, industry benchmarks)

## 4. Stock Photo Backlink Strategy

- Take original photos or create original images (charts, infographics, product shots) relevant to your niche
- Upload to free stock sites (Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay, Flickr Creative Commons) -- these are DR 90+ domains
- In the attribution/credit fields, link back to your website
- Every time a blogger, journalist, or designer uses your photo and credits you, you get a free backlink with zero outreach
- Periodically reverse-image-search your photos via Google Images or TinEye to find uncredited usage
- Email site owners: "Love that you used my image -- would you mind adding a source credit link?"
- Conversion rate is very high because the owner already used your content and knows they owe attribution
- One commenter reported unexpected backlink volumes just from uploading blog images to free stock sites

## 5. Press Release & Journalist Outreach Strategy

- Use ChatGPT Agent Mode: paste your landing page text, ask for pitch angles journalists in your niche would report on
- Follow up: "Give me a customized prompt for ChatGPT Agent Mode to find journalists to message"
- Agent Mode creates a virtual browser, spends 20+ minutes researching, returns a list of relevant journalists
- Then: "Analyze their last five articles and suggest a customized pitch angle for each"
- Have it write the pitches; edit for your personal voice, remove AI giveaways (em-dashes, buzzwords)
- Send through publicly listed emails or social DMs
- After getting covered, follow up every 1-2 months: "Anything I can comment on that you're writing?"
- Journalists often write for multiple publications -- one relationship yields links from multiple domains
- Featured.com / Help a Reporter Out / Source of Sources: respond to 1 journalist query per day
- Prioritize Source of Sources (best journalist quality), then HARO, then Featured
- Lead with a one-sentence credential, then give the journalist exactly what they asked for
- Identify the angle the journalist wants and confirm it -- acceptance rates of 76%+ are achievable
- Featured.com also offers a "Find Journalists" AI tool that searches 33,000+ journalists by topic

## 6. Podcast Guesting for Links

- Many podcast hosts syndicate episodes to their websites with show notes -- your backlink is in the description
- Hosts often accept your exact blurb, giving you full control over anchor text and brand language
- Transcripts on host sites create additional contextual mentions of your URL
- Podcast appearances on YouTube also create links; Google sees engagement signals on YouTube-hosted links
- Going on podcasts drives branded searches, which boosts SEO independently of the link itself
- Use podcast matching platforms (~$64/month) to get matched with relevant hosts
- A simple outreach message ("Hey, you game?") can be surprisingly effective
- 30 podcasts in 3 months is achievable; each compounds over time
- Podcasts in adjacent niches create partnership referral opportunities and reciprocal links

## 7. Listicle Outreach

- Self-promotional listicles (e.g., "Best X tools" where you rank yourself #1) still influence AI citations and rank well
- Keep volume to a handful (countable on one hand) -- sites publishing hundreds are getting penalized
- Be transparent: "We're biased, but here's why we're best" reads as authentic
- Don't write them with AI, don't just slap "2026" in the title without updating content
- Combine with diverse link building so your profile looks natural

## 8. Strategic Domain Acquisitions

- A $3,500 domain purchase with backlinks from major sites (Zapier, New York Post, Webflow) drove exponential traffic growth
- Key move: redirect acquired domain links to your most important pages, not just the homepage
- For specific tool-page backlinks, redirect to the matching page on your site
- The traffic spike was directly correlated to the day redirects were made
- Even small acquisitions can yield 10x monthly revenue increases

## 9. Directory Listings as Foundation

- If you are a real business, directory submissions are a natural activity -- Google expects this
- Submit to niche-specific directories AND general directories (LinkedIn, Crunchbase, Alternative.to, etc.)
- Track each submission in a spreadsheet: email, username, password, description text
- Update all directory descriptions when your business direction changes significantly
- Dozens of directories exist for every niche -- the bulk should be done at launch, with ongoing additions
- Profile links on Featured.com (nofollow, DA 41) and Favicon.com (dofollow, DA 44) are quick wins
- Grokopedia (DA 57) provides dofollow reference links -- but you need to get your entry indexed

## 10. Sweat Equity Partnerships

- Instead of paying for exposure, trade time/skills/expertise for access to someone's audience
- Build free tools, write content, or create co-branded assets for partners who host them on their site
- Lead with value: solve a real problem the partner has already publicly mentioned
- Example pitch: "I noticed you recommend X calculator but wished it had Y feature -- I just built a version with that feature"
- The pitch for partnership comes naturally if you actually make their life better
- Linkable assets (viral tools, interactive calculators, quizzes) are the highest-ROI link building method
- DoNothingFor2Minutes.com: a simple 2-minute mindfulness timer earned 7,100 linking domains and 384,000 backlinks
- Build linkable assets with vibe coding tools in a weekend; launch on Product Hunt and Betalist
- Host on your root domain or subdomain so all link equity flows to your main site
- Create research report linkable assets: aggregate interesting studies on a topic, write with a unique voice, cite sources with reverence so those cited will share your content
- Come up with a shocking aggregate stat for the headline (e.g., "Data reveals 89% of X are caused by Y")
- The people or companies cited in your research report become your first promoters -- they share it because you featured them positively

## 11. Link Building Economics

- Good PR agencies charge per published article; expect to pay meaningful amounts for Forbes, Nasdaq, GeekWire-level placements
- Paid link building budget: $1,000-$1,500/month gets quality links that move rankings in 6 months (zero to DR 50, 10K+ traffic)
- $5 links in YMYL niches are risky -- Google holds health/finance to higher trust standards
- The biggest ROI from PR comes not from the initial traffic burst, but from easing downstream conversions -- one B2B SaaS reported their sales nurturing cycle shortened dramatically after press coverage
- Linkable assets (viral tools, research reports) can generate 7,000+ linking domains at near-zero cost
- The best backlinks generate referral traffic that converts -- not just link juice

## 12. Don'ts and Risks

- Link ghosts cut both ways: negative effects of bad backlinks may also persist after removal
- Guest blogging purely for links is dead per Google (since 2014) -- only do it if the link brings relevant traffic or the publication adds credibility to your brand
- Never buy links at scale without diversifying your profile -- all-high-authority links look just as unnatural as all-low-authority
- A natural profile mixes: high-DR press, medium-DR niche blogs, low-DR real hobbyist sites, directories, social profiles, podcast mentions
- If you only do one type of link building, you are wasting time and potentially hurting yourself
- Do not use nofollow to "sculpt" PageRank internally -- Google confirmed in 2009 that blocked PageRank simply evaporates rather than being redistributed
- Don't close comments or avoid linking out to "hoard" authority -- Google's systems encourage links to good sites
- Avoid submitting AI-generated expert quotes to journalist platforms -- platforms now auto-detect and ban offenders
- Don't chase links without branded searches -- getting tons of links with zero brand searches looks suspicious
- DMCA / black-hat image claiming (uploading others' unclaimed images as your own) exists but carries reputational risk
