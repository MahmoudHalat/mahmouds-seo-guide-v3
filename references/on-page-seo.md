# On-Page SEO Reference

## 1. Search Intent Matching

**The golden rule**: if you don't match intent, nothing else matters -- you will never rank.

**Verification process**:
- Google your target keyword and study the top 10 results
- Identify the dominant content type: product page, blog post, comparison, listicle, forum thread, tool
- Check common content depth: do top pages go deep on one subtopic or cover breadth of related subtopics?
- Note rich media elements top pages use: embedded videos, images, graphics, tables, calculators
- Look at People Also Ask, related searches, and AI overviews for clues about what searchers specifically want

**Intent matching checklist**:
- Content format matches SERP majority (don't bring a blog post to a product-page fight)
- H1 and first 3 sentences directly address the searcher's core question
- The primary keyword appears in H1 and beginning of the first sentence
- Searcher should feel "this is exactly what I was looking for" within 3-5 seconds

**The SERP gap test**: look for keywords where no result has the keyword in the page title + URL slug -- that is a keyword gap, and merely targeting it properly gives you a competitive edge regardless of domain authority

## 2. E-E-A-T Signals (Practical)

E-E-A-T = Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness. Not a direct ranking signal but feeds into algorithmic quality scoring.

- **Experience**: first-person language ("we tested," "after building"), original screenshots/data, real scenarios
- **Expertise**: cover edge cases and nuances only a practitioner knows; use technical terms correctly; go beyond top-3 results
- **Authoritativeness**: author byline with credentials; link to LinkedIn from author bio; topical depth across multiple related pages
- **Trustworthiness**: cite sources for claims; multiple contact methods; client logos, ratings, awards; HTTPS + privacy policy; for YMYL topics credentials are mandatory
- **Off-site**: reputation (unlinked brand mentions, branded searches, sentiment) matters as much as backlinks; Google samples site quality -- a few terrible pages drag down the whole domain

## 3. Content Quality Scoring Rubric

6 weighted dimensions, each scored 0-10:

| Dimension | Weight | 9-10 Standard |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Search Intent Alignment | 20% | Format perfectly matches intent |
| E-E-A-T Signals | 20% | Author credentials, original data, cited claims |
| Content Quality and Depth | 20% | Comprehensive, original insight, anticipates follow-ups |
| On-Page SEO | 15% | Keyword in title + H1 + first sentence; proper heading hierarchy |
| Content Structure and UX | 15% | Short paragraphs, bullets, descriptive H2s, visual breaks every 300-500 words |
| Technical SEO | 10% | Fast load, mobile-ready, schema markup, no crawl errors |

**Anchors**: 2/10 = auto-generated template; 5/10 = decent but no original insight; 8/10 = expert guide with data + schema; 10/10 = industry-defining, cited by others

**Study data**: content quality correlates at 0.28 with rankings (backlinks: 0.17). Aim for 70-85 score, not 100.

## 4. Keyword Placement Rules (7 Locations)

Place your exact primary keyword in these locations (use it once per location; more = keyword stuffing):

1. **Page title (title tag)**: front-loaded, within first 30 characters; 50-60 chars total
2. **URL slug**: short, keyword-rich (e.g., /blog/seo-title-tags)
3. **H1 heading**: single H1 per page, contains primary keyword
4. **First sentence**: keyword at the beginning of the first sentence on the page
5. **Meta description**: keyword present naturally; 120-160 characters total
6. **Image alt text**: at least one image with descriptive alt text containing the keyword
7. **One or two H2 subheadings**: use primary or close secondary keyword naturally in 1-2 H2s

**Word count guidance**: long enough to cover the topic thoroughly but no longer. Removing text-length factors from scoring actually improved ranking correlation. Focus on topical coverage (at least 50% of key terms) rather than word count targets.

## 5. Title Tag Formula Library

| # | Formula | Template | CTR Lift |
|---|---------|----------|----------|
| 1 | How-To + Year | How to [Action] in [Year] | +10-15% |
| 2 | Number List | [N] [Adjective] [Topic] for [Audience] | +20-30% |
| 3 | Question + Answer | [Question]? Here's [Answer Preview] | +14% |
| 4 | Comparison | [A] vs [B]: [Differentiator] | high commercial |
| 5 | Best-of + Year | Best [Topic] in [Year] ([Qualifier]) | +10-15% |
| 6 | Parenthetical | [Topic] ([Modifier]) e.g. (With Templates) | +10-38% |
| 7 | Negative/Mistakes | [N] [Topic] Mistakes [Consequence] | high curiosity |
| 8 | Benefit-Driven | [Benefit]: [How to Achieve It] | strongest conversion |
| 9 | Keyword-Benefit-Brand | Keyword \| Searcher's Goal \| Brand | trust + priming |

**Benchmarks**: pos 1 = 29.3% CTR; pos 2 = 16.3%; pos 3 = 10.8%; pos 4-5 = 5.5-7.5%

**A/B test priority**: (1) add number +15-25%; (2) add year +10-15%; (3) add brackets +10-38%; (4) power word +5-12%; (5) reorder keyword +5-10%. Title must match content -- clickbait causes bounce that hurts rankings.

## 6. Meta Description Framework

**AIDA**: Attention (bold claim/question) + Interest (specifics) + Desire (benefit + proof) + Action (CTA). Example: "Want to double your organic traffic? This guide covers 15 proven strategies used by top-ranking sites. Read the full guide now."

**PAS**: Problem (pain point) + Agitate (amplify) + Solution (offer + CTA). Example: "Struggling to rank? Most SEO guides are outdated. Our 2026 guide covers what actually works. Read now."

**Specs**: 120-160 chars; include primary keyword; well-crafted descriptions matching intent get used as-is by Google. Adding a TLDR summary to article tops boosted conversions by 33% -- apply the same principle here.

## 7. Internal Linking Strategy

**Core principle**: internal links are not free authority -- each link is an investment; spend them judiciously

**Rules**:
- Every page links to the pillar/money page it supports and 2-3 related sibling pages
- Links must be contextual (woven into body text), not in blogrolls, footers, or nav (those are heavily devalued)
- Limit to ~5 in-body links for low-authority pages; scale with page length and type
- Use descriptive, keyword-relevant anchor text -- never "click here" or naked URLs
- No orphan pages: every page needs at least one inbound internal link

**Monthly audit process**:
1. Check Google Search Console Pages tab (last 7 days)
2. Filter for pages at position 4-10 (striking distance)
3. Add internal links from your highest-traffic pages pointing to these striking-distance pages
4. Remove links pointing to pages that can't rank (KD 90+ on a low-authority site = wasted link equity)
5. When a cluster page starts ranking, redirect some of its link equity to the next target

**The circuit-board analogy**: each ranking page generates authority like a battery; internal links channel that authority to new pages; but routing too many links from one page dilutes the signal -- reassess and rewire monthly

## 8. Content Structure for AI Extractability

AI systems and featured snippets need structured, scannable content to cite you:

- **Headings**: clear, natural language reflecting actual user questions (not clever/vague)
- **Answer speed**: key information presented early on the page, in plain language
- **Lists and tables**: use wherever relevant -- AI tools and featured snippets love extractable formats
- **FAQ sections**: structured question-and-answer pairs increase People Also Ask visibility
- **Schema markup**: Article, FAQ, HowTo, Organization as appropriate
- **Image alt text**: descriptive alt text on every image
- **Short paragraphs**: 2-3 lines max; most users scan, not read
- **Descriptive H2s**: searchers use H2s to decide if the page is for them -- make them clear
- **Above the fold**: answer the core question before the user scrolls; 60-70% of visitors never scroll

**For AI search citation**: structure evaluative content (FIFO vs LIFO, product comparisons) with clear verdict statements; AI synthesizes recommendations from decision-focused content with tight, quotable material

## 9. Cannibalization Detection and Resolution

**What it is**: multiple pages competing for the same keyword, causing the wrong page to rank or splitting authority

**When to worry**: large sites; a low-converting page outranks a high-converting one. **When NOT to worry**: small sites (<50 pages); deliberate UGC across platforms (controls more SERP real estate)

**Detection**: track keyword-to-page mapping in SEO tool; compare to actual rankings; check GSC for multiple URLs on same query

**Resolution** (order of preference):
1. Improve the page that's already ranking (optimize for conversions)
2. Consolidate duplicates into one page; 301 redirect removed URLs
3. Canonical tags for near-duplicates that must coexist
4. Hierarchical URL architecture to differentiate
5. Publish fresh UGC to replace bad-performing UGC (freshness is a strong signal)

Never noindex ranking pages -- almost always the wrong fix.

## 10. "Last Click" Quality Test

The single most important quality check before publishing:

**The test**: after reading this page, would the searcher need to go back to Google and try another result? If yes, the content fails regardless of all other signals.

**Positive signals** (content passes):
- Has a clear, specific target audience -- not "everyone"
- Answers the search query directly and completely
- Provides original value: analysis, insight, data -- not just compilation of other articles
- Demonstrates first-hand knowledge of the topic
- Has satisfying depth -- not thin, not padded with fluff

**Negative signals** (content fails):
- Summarizes others' content without adding value
- Uses excessive hedging ("it depends," "many factors") without commitments
- Promises an answer in the title but doesn't deliver it
- Covers a topic outside the site's core expertise
- Feels automated or mass-produced
- Has block paragraphs burying important information in walls of text

**Practical checks**:
- Read your own page as if you searched the keyword cold -- are you satisfied?
- Compare your page to the current #1 result -- do you offer something they don't?
- Check pogo-sticking potential: if 98 out of 100 visitors hit back, your page isn't satisfying users
- Effort does not equal quality (effort justification fallacy) -- the only measure is user satisfaction
- The above-the-fold section must answer three questions in 3-5 seconds: (1) What is this page? (2) Is this for me? (3) What should I do next?
