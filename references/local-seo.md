# Local SEO Reference

## 1. Google Business Profile Optimization

- Fix GBP BEFORE touching the website -- GBP drives 60-70% of local leads for most service businesses
- Primary category is the #1 local ranking factor -- never go broad (use "Personal Injury Attorney" not "Law Firm")
- Seasonal category switching: HVAC companies should swap primary category from "Furnace Repair" in fall to "AC Repair" in spring -- massive ranking shift
- Fill every field: services, hours, categories, description, products section, Q&A (Q&A being deprecated 2026)
- Upload real photos, never stock -- Google can identify stock images used across sites; aim for 10+ photos minimum, add 1/month
- Post weekly updates (250-300 chars + image) -- treat like a social post; signals active business to Google
- Use the Products section for services even though Google technically discourages it -- high visibility, links to individual service pages on your site
- Add exact-match low-competition services to the Services section -- can jump from position 20+ to top 3 in your area
- Any GBP edit triggers a full crawl of your website -- use this strategically when you publish new pages
- Keywords in business name still heavily weighted; getting a DBA with keywords + city is a legal cheat code that Google does not penalize
- Business description should be keyword-rich; use AI to weave keywords naturally into descriptions

## 2. Reviews Strategy

- Review velocity matters more than total count -- Google uses monthly new reviews as a popularity signal since it cannot see your CRM
- Benchmark competitor review velocity (e.g., 10/month) and communicate that target to the business owner
- Incentivize technicians $10-30 per review where their name is mentioned by the customer
- Ask customers to search your business name on Google first, THEN leave the review -- reviews that follow a search/engagement stick better
- Reviews left via QR codes on-premise are getting flagged; Google cracking down on on-premise review patterns with 30-day review blocks
- Never spike reviews (e.g., 30 in one day from a grand opening) -- abnormal volume triggers filtering
- Steady cadence: 2/week is often enough depending on industry; restaurants need more
- Focus on review CONTENT for AI/LLM recommendations -- detailed reviews with keywords help AI extract and recommend
- Menu item naming matters: "Caesar Kitchen" in GBP menu won't rank even with 34 keyword-rich reviews; changing to "Caesar Salad" caused rankings to surge
- Top-layer signals (category, services, menu items) must contain the keyword FIRST -- reviews reinforce but don't replace these primary signals
- Report extortion reviews via Google's dedicated form; never pay the ransom

## 3. Google Maps Ranking Factors (2026 WhiteSpark Survey)

- #1: Primary category on GBP
- #2: Proximity of address to point of search -- you cannot rank 50+ miles away without a new physical location
- Service areas set in GBP do NOT impact rankings -- they only show customers where you travel
- Grid ranking tools show your visibility radius; expand it with prominence signals (reviews, links, mentions)
- Keywords in owner review responses do NOT affect local algorithm -- respond like a human, not an SEO
- Review location signal is weak but fake reviews from distant locations can trigger spam flags
- The "diversity update" can cause GBP and homepage to compete -- consider a dedicated GBP landing page if you notice suppression
- Blue links below the local pack are buried under ads + pack + PAA + carousels -- focus on the pack first

## 4. Service Area Pages (vs. Doorway Pages)

- Service area pages rank in organic results (including AI/LLM results) but NOT in the local map pack
- To rank in the map pack for a distant city, you must open a physical location with a verified GBP
- Cheapest office: sublease via Craigslist/Facebook Marketplace ($400/month vs. $5K for own lease); must have signage on door/building
- Avoid PO boxes (against guidelines), virtual offices (risky), co-working (gray area unless dedicated lease + signage)
- Build location silos: /denver/drain-cleaning, /denver/water-heater-repair -- each hyper-focused on that city
- Do NOT copy-paste Aurora content with "Denver" swapped in -- rewrite each page; interview the business owner, transcribe their voice for authentic content
- Hub pages per location allow targeted link building; link equity from the hub flows to all child service pages
- New businesses: do NOT make distant service area pages yet -- build authority in your immediate area first, then expand 5-20 miles at a time
- Homepage must serve all locations once you expand; create a "Find a Location" hub page

## 5. Local Keyword Strategy

- Target hyper-specific purchase-intent keywords: "emergency brake repair in Dallas same day service honest pricing"
- These pages convert because searchers are desperate and ready to pay NOW
- Pages only need 400-500 words -- no fluff explaining what the service is; lead with why you are the right choice
- Use Google Search Console AI feature: "show me queries with local intent" reveals local keywords you rank for but haven't optimized
- Also try: "show me comparison and evaluation queries" and "show me service-based searches"
- Take queries you rank poorly for, add them as new sections or create dedicated pages
- Even high-CPC keywords like "personal injury lawyer NYC" ($200+/click) have low-authority sites ranking -- optimize properly and you compete
- Formula for big purchase orders: [service] + "for" + [plural modifier] (e.g., "cybersecurity audits for clinics")
- Niche down on location: target Plano, Irving, Garland before targeting "nationwide" vanity terms

## 6. Local Link Building

- Partnerships: plumber links to electrician, electrician links to plumber -- these pass real referral traffic
- Sponsorships: search `inurl:sponsors [city name]` on Google (or Yahoo for deeper index) to find opportunities; prioritize do-follow links
- Use AI agent mode to find sponsorship and partnership opportunities -- provide your business details, have it search local nonprofits, events, schools, sports leagues, HOAs, libraries, charities
- Citations that matter: Google Business Profile, Bing Places, Apple Business Connect, Yelp, Facebook, BBB, Yellow Pages, Data Axel, Foursquare -- then niche-specific ones
- Track all citations in a spreadsheet including blurb language -- vary blurbs strategically
- Semantic triples in blurbs: "Bob's Plumbing is a Denver plumbing company" -- repeat this pattern across sources to build entity understanding
- Press releases: max once per quarter for small businesses; NEVER use anchor text links (core updates destroying sites that do this); brand mention only
- Anchor text links at scale are now dangerous -- December 2025 core update wiped out small businesses doing this; cap at 1-5 total anchored links
- Guest posting on sites filled with guest posts = worthless; write for a real local blog that has topical authority
- Acquiring another brand's domain and redirecting it is an SEO goldmine -- point links strategically to location hub pages
- Awards and earned media (journalist quotes via HARO/Source of Sources) are safe, scalable, no anchor text risk

## 7. Local Blog Content Strategy

- Write content that feels like it came from the neighborhood: "How to protect your garage door before Houston storms destroy it"
- Use street names, local landmarks, seasonal events -- not generic "tips for homeowners"
- Local blogs attract local news publishers who want expert quotes during storm season, heat waves, etc.
- Blogs drive internal links to money pages -- Joy Hawkins confirmed a single internal link from a high-traffic blog moved local pack rankings significantly
- Blog pages should link to service pages; service pages link back to blogs; homepage links to both -- this loop builds topical authority
- Delete or consolidate thin/outdated blog content -- index bloat, keyword overlap, and click decay hurt rankings

## 8. Geotagging and Location Signals

- 10-week study on 27 businesses: geotagging images with EXIF coordinates does NOT help city-name queries (actually decreased rankings)
- "Near me" queries saw a small positive lift in the geotagged target area -- but rankings dropped everywhere else
- Net effect: geotagging hurts more than it helps; agency recommendation is to skip it entirely
- Google's John Mueller said geotagging is unnecessary for SEO
- Driving directions hack: using Google Maps driving directions to your business signals demand -- works but stopping causes rankings to drop (withdrawal effect); risky and spammy, not recommended for clients

## 10. Local Business AI Lead Generation

- Build FAQ silos from AI prompt gaps: use AI visibility tools to find prompts your business does NOT appear in
- Group those prompts by topic/location, create nested FAQ pages, interlink them
- Case study: walk-in tub installer went from low AI visibility to first AI-generated lead in 2 weeks
- Case study: pool company went from 36% to 100% AI visibility score in 2 months using FAQ silo method
- For LLMs, service area pages are valuable even without map pack ranking -- AI chatbots reference and recommend from these pages
- Business name consistency across your site is critical for AI-powered local packs (even capitalization matters)
- Reviews with keyword-rich content help LLMs extract and recommend your business
- Focus on review content quality over quantity for AI/LLM recommendations

## 11. Local SEO Checklist

- [ ] Audit and fix GBP: primary category, secondary categories, all photos real, services listed with descriptions, hours accurate
- [ ] Collect 10+ reviews in first week; implement review cadence via post-job text/email; incentivize technicians ($10-30/review if their name mentioned)
- [ ] Do NOT use QR codes for review collection (Google cracking down on on-premise reviews; 30-day review blocks)
- [ ] Ask reviewers to search your business name first, then leave review (sticks better than direct link)
- [ ] Build hyper-specific money pages (400-500 words each) for every service + location combination
- [ ] Create services hub page linked from footer; organize services by category with H2s
- [ ] Fix internal linking: service pages <-> blogs <-> homepage in logical loops
- [ ] Run technical audit with Screaming Frog (free up to 500 URLs): fix broken links, duplicate titles, missing H1s, redirect chains, orphan pages
- [ ] Set up core citations (10 platforms); track in spreadsheet with blurb language
- [ ] Build local links: partnerships, sponsorships, earned media, local blog writing
- [ ] Post weekly on GBP; reply to all reviews authentically
- [ ] Use GSC AI features to discover unoptimized local queries
- [ ] Build FAQ silos targeting AI prompt gaps for AI/LLM visibility
- [ ] Report negative review extortion via Google's dedicated form -- never pay
- [ ] Counter competitor GBP spam via redressal form (not suggested edits -- legal risk of subpoena)
